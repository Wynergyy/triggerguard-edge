PAYMENT → LICENCE FLOW

TriggerGuard – Wynergy Fibre Solutions Ltd
Authoritative Commercial & Audit Process

1. Purpose

This document defines the authoritative, auditable process linking customer payment to TriggerGuard licence issuance.
It removes ambiguity, prevents false sales, and mirrors enterprise SaaS practice used by major vendors.

This process applies to:

TriggerGuard Pilot

TriggerGuard Sentinel+

Future TriggerGuard tiers

2. Parties and Responsibilities

Customer

Completes payment via authorised Tide payment link

Provides a valid business email address

Tide (Payment Provider)

Processes payment

Issues payment confirmation and reference

Acts as the financial record of sale

Does not issue licences

Wynergy Fibre Solutions Ltd

Licence authority and issuer

Maintains licence ledger

Sends official licence communications

Enforces expiry and renewal

TriggerGuard System

Generates deterministic licences

Verifies licences at runtime

Enforces expiry and tier controls

3. Authoritative Flow (Live Sales)

Customer completes payment via Tide payment link

Tide generates:

Payment confirmation

Unique payment reference

Payment reference is treated as proof of entitlement

Licence is issued using:

Customer email

Payment reference

Product tier

Licence is recorded in the authoritative licence ledger

Customer receives:

Licence confirmation email

Licence details

Expiry date

Verification instructions

Renewal schedule is activated

No licence exists without a valid payment reference.

4. Test and Internal Issuance Rules

To prevent false sales:

Test licences must not use live Tide references

Test references must be clearly marked:

TEST-REF-*

INTERNAL-*

Test licences:

Are excluded from revenue reporting

May use shortened expiry

Must never be shared externally

Live customer licences must always reference a real Tide or contract reference.

5. Licence Characteristics

Each licence includes:

Unique licence ID

Product name

Tier (Pilot, Sentinel+, etc.)

Status (Active, Expired, Revoked)

Customer email

Payment reference

Issue timestamp

Expiry timestamp

Cryptographic integrity hash

Issuer identity

Licences are time-limited by default.
No lifetime licences are issued unless explicitly defined by contract.

6. Expiry and Renewal

Pilot licences default to fixed-term duration

Sentinel+ licences follow contract terms

Renewal notices are issued prior to expiry

Expired licences:

Fail verification

Are rejected at runtime

Require renewal to reactivate

7. Automation Boundary (Future)

Current state:

Manual issuance using deterministic tooling

Future state (planned):

Tide webhook ingestion

Automatic licence issuance

Automated email delivery

Automated renewal reminders

This document remains authoritative regardless of automation level.

8. Audit and Integrity

This flow ensures:

Clear financial → entitlement linkage

Deterministic licence generation

Verifiable audit trail

Enterprise-grade compliance posture

This mirrors accepted practice across major SaaS vendors.
